# OctoAcme New Team Member Onboarding Checklist

## Purpose
This checklist ensures new team members have a consistent, thorough onboarding experience and can quickly become productive contributors.

## Instructions
- The Onboarding Coordinator owns this checklist and tracks completion
- Complete items are marked with `[x]`, pending items with `[ ]`
- Customize this template based on the role and project context
- Check in at key milestones: end of week 1, end of week 2, 30 days, 90 days

---

## Pre-Day 1 (Onboarding Coordinator)
- [ ] Send welcome email with start date, time, and location details
- [ ] Coordinate with IT for laptop, accounts, and access setup
- [ ] Add to team chat channels and distribution lists
- [ ] Assign onboarding buddy/mentor
- [ ] Schedule first-week meetings (1:1s, team intro, orientation)
- [ ] Prepare new hire workspace (if applicable)

---

## Day 1 — Welcome & Orientation
- [ ] Meet with manager for welcome and overview
- [ ] Complete IT setup (laptop, accounts, VPN, security training)
- [ ] Introduce to team members and buddy
- [ ] Review team structure, org chart, and key contacts
- [ ] Overview of OctoAcme mission, values, and culture
- [ ] Tour of office/virtual workspace norms
- [ ] Access to core tools (GitHub, project boards, communication tools)

---

## Week 1 — Foundations
### Administrative
- [ ] Complete HR paperwork and onboarding modules
- [ ] Set up benefits and payroll (if applicable)
- [ ] Review employee handbook and code of conduct

### Technical & Role-Specific
- [ ] Clone key repositories and verify development environment
- [ ] Review OctoAcme documentation (project management overview, roles, processes)
- [ ] Shadow team members in daily standup and key meetings
- [ ] Complete first small task or "good first issue"
- [ ] Review current sprint goals and roadmap

### Communication & Culture
- [ ] Meet with Product Manager for product vision overview
- [ ] Meet with Project Manager to understand project status and workflows
- [ ] Attend team standup and introduce yourself
- [ ] Schedule recurring 1:1 with manager
- [ ] Review communication norms (when to use chat vs. email vs. meetings)

---

## Week 2-4 — Ramp Up
- [ ] Complete role-specific training (e.g., code review standards, testing practices)
- [ ] Take on small-to-medium complexity tasks
- [ ] Participate in sprint planning and retrospectives
- [ ] Attend cross-functional meetings relevant to role
- [ ] Review architecture and technical documentation
- [ ] Understand deployment process and release cadence
- [ ] Meet with QA Lead to understand quality standards (if applicable)
- [ ] Meet with Release Manager to understand release process (if applicable)

---

## 30-Day Check-In
- [ ] 1:1 with manager to discuss progress, questions, and feedback
- [ ] Review role expectations and goals for first 90 days
- [ ] Identify any gaps in knowledge or training needs
- [ ] Provide feedback on onboarding experience (survey or discussion)
- [ ] Confirm clarity on team priorities and ways of working

---

## 60-90 Days — Full Integration
- [ ] Independently complete medium-to-high complexity tasks
- [ ] Contribute to design discussions and code reviews
- [ ] Mentor or support newer team members (if applicable)
- [ ] Take ownership of a feature or component area
- [ ] Present work or learnings to the team
- [ ] Complete any remaining compliance or security training

---

## 90-Day Review
- [ ] Formal check-in with manager for performance and goal alignment
- [ ] Reflect on achievements and areas for growth
- [ ] Provide feedback to improve onboarding process
- [ ] Set goals for next quarter
